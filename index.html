About

Daniela Gonzalez is a graduate student completing her thesis in the Anthropology Department at the California State University of Long Beach. She has experience in board governance and program development working with a 501(c)3. She has implemented DEI initiatives to foster positive change, cultivate inclusive environments, and diversify board member recruitment. Her dedication to DEI, collaboration, and stakeholder engagement has led to improved relations and overall organizational success. Daniela is currently completing her master's degree in Applied Anthropology at California State University, Long Beach. She spends her free time trail running, throwing pottery, and watching films.
As Daniela approaches graduation, she is enthusiastic about the upcoming professional chapter. She will actively seek meaningful opportunities within the DEI sector beginning this Spring. Driven by a passion for making a positive impact, she looks forward to contributing her skills and dedication to fostering inclusive environments.

Daniela is currently a contracted workshop facilitator with the Long Beach based DEI consulting firm, Willing Observers.

Current Projects

Master's Thesis

Her study explores a university's organizational culture through ethnographic interviews to inform the design of academic resources and strategies to increase a sense of belonging on a campus. Preliminary findings identify opportunities for growth in academic resources, despite the university's stated efforts and emphasis on belonging and community. Both students and staff leverage academic resources for building relationships, with negative impressions shaping student resource-seeking behaviors. Using anthropological methods, her study aims to provide actionable recommendations aligned with current student needs, emphasizing the pivotal role of belonging in DEI initiatives. This research underscores the practical application of anthropological insights in organizational strategies to foster student success. She is presenting at the Annual Conference for the Society for Applied Anthropology this March 2024.

Ceramics

Daniela began her ceramics journey during 2020 pandemic. This coincided with her undergraduate studies, where she found solace and inspiration in her first handbuilding course. As she transitioned into graduate school, her passion for ceramics deepened, leading her to learn the art of wheel throwing. While Daniela solidified her commitment to understanding, supporting, and building communities she also discovered a profound connection between her love for ceramics. The wedging table and wheel became not just tools for crafting clay but symbolic spaces where patience and commitment translated into beautiful forms. Daniela has established her home studio. Daniela is on a mission to define her unique artistic style through clay forms. She aims to share a narrative of clay and community, where each piece tells a story of patience, commitment, and the beauty that emerges when diverse elements come together.
